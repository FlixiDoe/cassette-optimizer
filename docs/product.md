# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cassette Optimizer is for people who record Spotify playlists onto physical cassette tapes and need the software to respect the physical workflow: tape length, deck timing, side changes, readiness checks, and the fact that mistakes waste real time and media. They may use the app at a desk during planning, then on a laptop or nearby phone-sized monitor while operating a cassette deck.

## Product Purpose

The product helps users turn a Spotify playlist into a cassette recording plan, control playback side by side, and produce printable J-cards. Success means the user can load a playlist, choose real tapes they own, verify readiness, start Side A and Side B with confidence, recover safely from reloads or rate limits, and avoid accidental Spotify playlist changes or unsafe recording starts.

## Brand Personality

Technical, focused, tactile. The interface should feel like a precise studio utility for a physical recording session: calm under pressure, direct about risk, and grounded in cassette hardware without becoming novelty retro decoration.

## Anti-references

Do not make the app look like generic AI slop: no decorative card grids, glassmorphism, gradient text, oversized marketing hero patterns, random glow effects, or ornamental UI that does not serve the recording workflow. It should not mimic Spotify's consumer brand, become a toy-like retro cassette theme, or read like a generic SaaS dashboard.

## Design Principles

1. Make readiness unmistakable: recording gates, blockers, and recovery paths must be more legible than decoration.
2. Respect the physical workflow: planning, tape inventory, deck setup, recording controls, and J-card output should map cleanly to what the user is doing with real hardware.
3. Keep controls reachable at the moment of use: recording actions must remain fast to scan and easy to hit, especially on smaller screens.
4. Prefer operational clarity over visual novelty: every visual flourish should clarify state, rhythm, or physical tape behavior.
5. Preserve local trust: secret handling, exports, imports, and destructive Spotify actions must stay explicit and reversible where possible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Keyboard users need visible focus, logical tab order, and a skip path to recording controls. Motion must respect reduced-motion preferences. Color should reinforce status but not be the only signal for readiness, warnings, errors, or recording prompts. Touch targets should remain usable on phone-sized screens during an active recording session.
